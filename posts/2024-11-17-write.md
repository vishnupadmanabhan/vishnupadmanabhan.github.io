Be it a blog, a journal, or even scattered thoughts in a notebook, write for yourself.

You might hear conflicting advice online. Ignore it.

Start with your thoughts. Answer the nagging questions in your head. Explore your curiosities, and document what you uncover. Tackle a problem and reflect on how you solved it.

That’s all you need to do.

When you write for yourself, you become your own audience, the most forgiving, curious, and honest reader you could ever have. And in that freedom, you’ll find clarity and joy.

Free yourself. Write for you.
